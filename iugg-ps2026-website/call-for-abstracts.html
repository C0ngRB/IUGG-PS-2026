Call for Abstracts

2nd IUGG Symposium on Planetary Sciences (IUGG-PS2026)
21–23 September 2026 · Wuhan, China

Scope

IUGG-PS2026 invites contributions from all fields of planetary sciences that advance our understanding of planetary environments within and beyond our Solar System, and that foster interdisciplinary collaboration across geology, atmospheric science, geophysics, geodesy, astronomy, geochemistry, astrobiology, and remote sensing.

Sessions & Topics

  • Session 1: Lunar Exploration and Science
  • Session 2: Mars Exploration and Science
  • Session 3: Asteroid Exploration and Science
  • Session 4: Extrasolar Planetary Systems
  • Session 5: Planetary Probes Navigation and Orbiting
  • Session 6: Planetary Geodesy and Remote Sensing
  • Session 7: Planetary Systems, Rotation and Fluid Dynamics
  • Session 8: Planetary Life and Astrobiology
  • Session 9: Comparative Planetology
  • Session 10: Future Planetary Missions and Sciences

Submission Guidelines

  • All abstracts must be submitted electronically through the conference registration and submission portal (link: iugg2026).
  • Each abstract is limited to one page and may include at most one figure.
  • Authors should clearly indicate the preferred session when submitting their abstract.
  • Abstracts will be reviewed by the Scientific Organizing Committee based on scientific merit, originality, and relevance to the symposium themes.
  • Accepted contributions will be assigned to oral or poster sessions depending on the overall program and time constraints.
  • The maximum poster size is 120 × 140 cm (H × L). A0 portrait posters will fit well.

Registration & Fees (Information)

Registration and payment for IUGG-PS2026 are handled via the conference registration system (link: iugg2026). The present website provides information only and does not implement any online registration forms.

  • Regular: 2000 Chinese Yuan
  • Students: 1000 Chinese Yuan
  • After registration, you can log in with your username and password to submit your abstract.
  • Each abstract is limited to 1 page with a maximum of 1 figure.
  • The maximum size of the poster is (H × L) = (120 × 140 cm). A0 portrait posters will fit well.

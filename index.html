About IUGG-PS2026

With the successful completion of recent lunar and Mars missions, a new era has begun for the observation and exploration of the Solar System. Planetary science is an increasingly interdisciplinary field propelled forward by advances in space exploration and ground-based observations.

The 2nd IUGG Symposium on Planetary Sciences (IUGG-PS2026) aims to bring together international scientists and engineers focused on interdisciplinary research on the exploration and science of the Solar System and the search for life beyond Earth. Topics include planetary geodesy, remote sensing, atmospheres, geomorphology, geophysics, geodynamics, geology, geochemistry, interior physics, and astrobiology. All objects from terrestrial and giant planets to small bodies and exoplanets are welcome.

Sessions & Topics

IUGG-PS2026 covers a broad range of topics on planetary sciences through themed sessions:

  • Session 1: Lunar Exploration and Science
  • Session 2: Mars Exploration and Science
  • Session 3: Asteroid Exploration and Science
  • Session 4: Extrasolar Planetary Systems
  • Session 5: Planetary Probes Navigation and Orbiting
  • Session 6: Planetary Geodesy and Remote Sensing
  • Session 7: Planetary Systems, Rotation and Fluid Dynamics
  • Session 8: Planetary Life and Astrobiology
  • Session 9: Comparative Planetology
  • Session 10: Future Planetary Missions and Sciences

For detailed scope and abstract guidelines, please visit the Call for Abstracts page.

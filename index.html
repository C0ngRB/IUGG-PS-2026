Interdisciplinary Observation and Understanding of the Solar System

September 21–23, 2026 · Wuhan, China

About IUGG-PS2026

With the successful completion of recent lunar and Mars missions, a new era has begun for the observation and exploration of the Solar System. Planetary science is an increasingly interdisciplinary field propelled forward by advances in space exploration and ground-based observations.

The 2nd IUGG Symposium on Planetary Sciences (IUGG-PS2026) aims to bring together international scientists and engineers focused on interdisciplinary research on the exploration and science of the Solar System and the search for life beyond Earth. Topics include planetary geodesy, remote sensing, atmospheres, geomorphology, geophysics, geodynamics, geology, geochemistry, interior physics, and astrobiology. All objects from terrestrial and giant planets to small bodies and exoplanets are welcome.

Scientific Program (Tentative)

A detailed program including plenary lectures, oral sessions and poster sessions will be announced in due course. A tentative structure is given below.

  • Day 1: Opening ceremony, keynote lectures, and sessions on lunar and Mars exploration.
  • Day 2: Sessions on small bodies, exoplanets, planetary geodesy and remote sensing.
  • Day 3: Sessions on planetary interiors, astrobiology, comparative planetology, future missions, and closing remarks.

The final program and detailed schedule will be posted on this page.

Call for Abstracts

Abstracts are invited for oral and poster presentations at IUGG-PS2026. Contributions are welcome on all aspects of planetary sciences, including but not limited to:

  • Lunar and Mars exploration and science;
  • Asteroids, comets and small bodies;
  • Exoplanets and planetary systems;
  • Planetary geodesy, gravity and remote sensing;
  • Planetary atmospheres and climate;
  • Interior structure and dynamics of planets;
  • Astrobiology and planetary habitability;
  • Future planetary missions and instrumentation.

Submission Guidelines

  • Abstract length: maximum 1 page in English (including figures and references);
